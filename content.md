### Why we exist

**Heading:** Why we exist

**Body (paragraph 1):** Most small and medium businesses handle payroll in one of two ways: they do it themselves — spending hours every month getting it roughly right — or they use a generic software package that requires constant attention to stay compliant.

**Body (paragraph 2):** Neither works well. HMRC's RTI system requires on-time, accurate submissions every single pay period. Statutory deductions — PAYE, NI, student loans, SMP, SSP, pensions — change constantly. A missed threshold or a late filing is a penalty.

**Body (paragraph 3):** Ackerman Pierce was built to take all of that off your desk. We are based in Wolverhampton, registered in England & Wales (Company No. 17018115), and VAT registered (No. 512993087). We work with UK businesses of all sizes.

### From data to payday, we own the whole process

**Heading:** From data to payday, we own the whole process

**Subheading:** Every arrow below is a task we handle on your behalf, every single pay period.

#### Phase: Input
- New starters & leavers notified — We process P45s and set up tax codes
- Hours, overtime & bonuses submitted — Via email, spreadsheet, or any format you use
- Absence & leave recorded — SSP, SMP, SPP automatically calculated

#### Phase: Processing
- Gross pay calculated per employee — Salaries, hourly rates, bonuses, commissions
- PAYE income tax deducted — Using HMRC-issued tax codes, updated in-year
- National Insurance calculated — Employee and employer contributions, correct NI category
- Student loan deductions applied — Plan 1, 2 and 4 — correct threshold per employee
- Pension contributions calculated — Auto-enrolment compliant, coordinated with your provider

#### Phase: Submission
- Full Employer Payment Summary sent — RTI filed with HMRC on or before payday
- You review & approve figures first — Nothing submitted until you sign off
- BACS payment file prepared — Salaries sent directly to employee bank accounts

#### Phase: Reporting & Compliance
- Payslips issued to all employees — Digital or printed — your choice
- Monthly payroll report delivered — Cost breakdown by employee and department
- P60s issued at year-end — For every active employee, on time
- P45s on day of leaving — Issued immediately when an employee leaves

## HMRC Penalty Reference (factual, sourced)

> **Late or incorrect RTI filings attract automatic penalties:**
> - £100 per month for 1 to 9 employees
> - £200 per month for 10 to 49 employees
> - £300 per month for 50 to 249 employees
> - £400 per month for 250 or more employees
>
> Source: Income Tax (PAYE) Regulations 2003 — HMRC in-year filing penalties.

> **P60 distribution** — due by 31 May each year. Failure: HMRC non-compliance notice.
> **Statutory Maternity Pay** — from week 11 before due date. Failure: employee rights claim.
> **Statutory Sick Pay** — from day 4 of absence. Failure: employment tribunal exposure.
